Credit Products

Personal Development Loans

  • Repayment period shall vary on case by case basis as determined by the board
  • A member must have been active and consistently saved with NEKDS for a period of not less than six (6) months
  • A member shall qualify for a loan with their savings without security as long as he or she has not used the same savings to guarantee any other loan
  • A member shall borrow up to three times his or her savings
  • The interest rate shall be 1% per month on the reducing balance or as shall be determined by the Board of Directors from time to time
  • Every loan shall attract a charge of 0.1% and shall be processed within a maximum of six (6) working days
  • Members will be required to not only provide security to qualify for loans but also demonstrate ability to make repayments
  • The total of all outstanding loans, including personal development loans, emergency and air ticket advance loans must not exceed three times a member’s total savings

Emergency Loans

  • The total of all outstanding loans, including emergency, air-ticket advance and development loans, must not exceed three times a member’s savings
  • Repayment period shall be six months
  • Emergency loans shall attract interest of 2.5% per month on reducing balance and a charge of 0.1%

Air-Ticket Advance Loans

  • The total of all outstanding loans, including emergency, air-ticket advance and development loans, must not exceed three times a member’s savings
  • Repayment period shall be six months
  • Air-Tickets Advanced loans shall attract interest of 3% per month on reducing balance and a charge of 0.1%

Savings Products

Equity Capital/Shares

  • Each NEKDS member is required to upon registration purchase at least one thousand (1000)shares at Kshs 100 per share or at such price as may vary from time to time. Shares shall be payable within 2 years of membership and shall not be refundable or assignable as collateral for a loan, but a member may transfer his or her shares to other members on leaving membership of the Sacco
  • Shares may earn dividends paid from net surplus after required transfers to reserves at the end of every financial year in accordance with the dividend policy of the NEKDS. A member in good standing should thus have at least Kshs 100,000 worth of shares

Investment Savings

  • These savings may be used as collateral against borrowings from the Sacco by an individual borrower or as guarantee for loans to other members and shall only be refunded when a member withdraws from membership provided the member has fully repaid all his or her loans and is free from guarantee
  • These savings shall be non-withdrawable and shall be contributed on a monthly basis. Each member shall contribute not less than Ksh.5,000 per month
  • Upon termination of membership, Non-withdrawable savings shall be refundable within sixty days after receiving a written notification from the member
  • Interest on savings shall be paid at a rate determined by the board at the end of the year after audit has been completed and shall thus vary from time to time

NEKDS Junior Savings Account

  • A member shall be free to open account for each of his or her children below the age of majority at will
  • Whereas the parent shall have the sole mandate to operate the accounts, every account shall bare the child’s name
  • NEKDS shall from time to time as may be determined by the Board of Directors prescribe the following in respect of NEKDS Junior Savings Account: The minimum amount required to open an account and the minimum balance to maintain it; The minimum interest-earning balance; The interest rate applicable; The penalty for early withdrawal or withdrawal above set limits, if any; and The maturity of a term account.
